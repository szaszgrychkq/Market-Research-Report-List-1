<p><p>The disintegrants market is segmented into natural and synthetic disintegrants. Natural disintegrants, derived from plant sources like starch and gums, offer biocompatibility and are often favored in formulations aimed at minimizing chemical additives. In contrast, synthetic disintegrants, such as sodium starch glycolate and croscarmellose sodium, provide enhanced efficiency and control over disintegration times. Both types play crucial roles in improving the bioavailability of medications by ensuring rapid tablet breakdown in the gastrointestinal tract, thereby enhancing drug release and absorption.</p></p>
